## Back to Bloggin'
Greetings all! ~3 months since my last blog post.  I'm gonna try to stick to blogging more consistently, but doing anything consistently has been a lifelong struggle for me. I'm doing my best!

So, what's been going on w/ lil ol me? Well, a lot and nothing at all. Intermittent updates to my site, quality of life changes, etc etc. I made a post feed on my [main page](https://kingposs.com/) that I write and update from Obsidian just as I do this blog. Since it's just short posts (like tweets or whatever), I should update it a lot more. I'm working on a password encrypted page that lets confidants and colleagues listen to my archived radio broadcasts, as well as resources and books I've gathered, all encrypted. If an encrypted webpage sounds cool to you, check out [PageCrypt](https://pagecrypt.lupine.dev/). More to come on that. 

I've been fishing more up at my wife and I's favorite lake! Though truthfully, not as much as I would like to. It brings me utter serenity, and I really, *really* need to make the effort to get up there more. We've been catching and cooking trout, and I recently figured out a more efficient way to gut and filet em! What used to take me an 1 hour 40 minutes, will take me just 40. I feel really good about that. I also got a pair of whetstones and learned how to properly sharpen a blade. Went and practiced on every blade we had in the house. A sharp blade really makes all the difference when you're filleting fish. Our preferred method of cooking is to batter and fry and eat em in po boy sandwiches. Simple and delicious every time :3 

## Learning to Live with Autism and ADHD
I got my autism and ADHD assessment report back from the medical records office some time in the last 3 months and I've been mulling over the results. It's a little weird seeing the events of your life layed before you in someone else's words. All (or a lot of) your deficits quantified with numbers and data points. What really surprised me was realizing I was diagnosed with level 2 autism, which states those diagnosed "require substantial support". That's been sitting hard with me and something I've really been trying to internalize.
